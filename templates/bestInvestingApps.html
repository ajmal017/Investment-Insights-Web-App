Best Investing Apps

In the past, investing in the stock market was something only high net-worth individuals can do and required having a stock broker. However, with the emergence of FinTech companies in the last decade, investing has never been more simple. Nowadays, all someone needs to invest in stocks is money to spare and a smartphone. There are multiple apps anyone can download to help reach their financial goals. Here are a few apps that can help you start investing for free.

Acorns

Acorns is the perfect app for passive or new investors, or people who do not have the time to pick individual stocks to invest in. The app is very simple, for every purchase you make with a linked credit or debit card, Acorns rounds the purchase up to the next dollar and invests it for you in a diversifed portfolio of assets that you choose. For example, if you buy a cup of coffee for $2.60, Acorns takes the 40 cents left over and invests it in your account. Even though it seems like a small amount of money, you would be surprised by how fast your money grows, especially if you make a ton of purchases. Apart from the round-ups feature, Acorns allows you to setup recurring investments, so you can transfer any amount of money every day, week, or month. It is the perfect way to save money without even thinking about it. Click here to learn more about Acorns.

Stash

Stash is very similar to Acorns, but it is more customizable and interactive. It is recommended for people who have some investing experience and understand how the stock market works. Stash has the same round-up feature as Acorns, and allows you to transfer any amount of money every week or month. Stash allows you to buy fractional shares of a stock, eliminating the need to gain enough capital to purchase full shares of a stock. It also allows you to purchase select ETFs for a certain category such as blue chip or conservative stocks, for instant diversification. Stash also features a section where you can read about the economy and for general financial advice. The app also recommends you stocks you should consider adding to your portfolio. With the Stash Debit Card, you can also earn "Stock-Back" from certain diners and venues. Click here to learn more about Stash.

Robinhood

Robinhood is geared towards millenials who have a general understanding of the stock market and are looking to invest without the burden of commission fees. Because every trade you make on Robinhood is free, you end up saving thousands of dollars over time by avoiding those costs. Robinhood allows you to invest in stocks, ETFs, and certain cryptocurrencies. This is the greatest benefit of Robinhood, as there are very few apps that allow users to invest in all 3 under the same platform. Robinhood also has a free stock program, where you can get a variety of stocks for free for referring a friend, up to $500. With Robinhood, you can view the average price other Robinhood users paid for all stocks and their analyst ratings. Robinhood Gold gives you get access to in-depth research from MorningStar and Level II Market Data from NASDAQ for $10 a month. It also large instant deposits, so you do not have to wait 3 days for your money to transfer to your Robinhood account. Click here to learn more about Robinhood.
